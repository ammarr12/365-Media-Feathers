# 365 Media Feathers - Social Media Agency Dashboard

## About the Project
I built a three-page interactive dashboard for 365 Media Feathers, a social media agency[cite: 5]. The goal of this project was to take marketing and sales data and break it down into clear, easy-to-read visuals so the team can see exactly which campaigns are working, who their biggest customers are, and where most of their sales are coming from.

Instead of hunting through spreadsheets, this layout gives a quick look at customer habits and marketing performance.

---

## Dashboard Pages

### 1. Campaign Performance
This page focuses on marketing results to show if ad campaigns are actually converting into sales.

* **Campaign Acceptance Rate:** Shows that 5.97% of target audiences accepted the marketing campaigns[cite: 5].
* **Top Performing Campaigns:** Tracks which specific campaigns generated the highest response, with Campaign 4 and Campaign 3 leading the pack[cite: 5].
* **Success by Country:** Breaks down campaign responses by location, showing Spain has the highest acceptance by a large margin[cite: 5].
* **Sales Channels:** A pie chart showing exactly how people buy—46.2% of sales happen via Store Purchases, 32.53% online via Web Purchases, and 21.27% through Catalog Purchases[cite: 5].

### 2. Product Sales Breakdown
This section tracks what products are moving the fastest and which countries buy the most.

* **Total Sales by Country:** Shows geographical revenue splits, with Spain bringing in the vast majority of sales at 63.54%, followed by Saudi Arabia (20.33%) and Canada (16.13%)[cite: 5].
* **Product Popularity:** A clear bar chart and tree map sorting products by value[cite: 5]. Wine products and meat products are the biggest revenue drivers, while fruits and sweets make up the smallest share[cite: 5].

### 3. Customer Demographics
This page answers the question of *who* is actually buying from the agency's clients by breaking down sales based on personal backgrounds.

* **Income Levels:** Compares buying power, showing that high-income customers are significantly more likely to make a purchase than medium-income buyers[cite: 5].
* **Education Level:** Breaks down total sales by the customer's education, revealing that people with a college Graduation background account for the biggest share of sales, followed by PhD holders and Master's graduates[cite: 5].
* **Marital Status:** Tracks buying habits by family status, showing that married individuals drive the most revenue[cite: 5].

## How to Interact with It
* **Sidebar Navigation:** Use the buttons on the left sidebar (Campaign, Product, Customer) to hop between different views[cite: 5].
* **Filters:** Clicking on any country, product type, or demographic bar automatically filters the rest of the charts on the page to match that selection[cite: 5].
* **Reset Button:** Use the "Reset All filters" link at the bottom of the sidebar to instantly clear selections and go back to the default view[cite: 5].
